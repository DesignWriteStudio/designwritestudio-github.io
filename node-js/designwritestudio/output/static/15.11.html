Selection, association, contiguity, and stratiﬁcation. In addition to being accessible by the above-mentioned modes, the elements of informa- tion can be distributed in two or three hierarchical levels accord- ing to their degree of complexity. This makes it possible to meet the needs of various categories of readers or to satisfy different informa- tion needs for a single reader. This hypertext model best combines the advantages of the codex with the possibilities opened up by the computer by taking into account a new dimension of the text, that of depth. By superimposing different layers of text on a single subject, or to use another metaphor, by encircling a central nucleus with vari- ous supplementary documents, the uses of which are well deﬁned, a stratiﬁed hypertext provides several books in one.
