Today, publishers make such effort to enable the reader to perceive com~ plete words that they sometimes hesitate to break a word at the end of a line, and thus to use justiﬁed text, although that was the typographical ideal for centuries, beginning in the time of the volumen. This concern with match ing the semantic unit with the unit of visual perception is also evident in magazines, which tend increasingly to make the text of articles ﬁt into the space of the page or double page.
