When the potential of this union of form and content in the page became apparent, various types of visual markers were gradually added to the organi- zation of the book to help readers ﬁnd their bearings more easily in the mass of text and make reading easier and more efﬁcient. Since the page constitutes a visual unit of information related to the preceding and the following pages, allowing it to be numbered and given a header, it has an autonomy that the column of text in the volumen did not. Thanks to the page, it is possible to leaf through a book and quickly know its contents, or at least the essentials.
