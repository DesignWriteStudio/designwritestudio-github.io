8.4

15th February 2018 at 9:54am
Annotator

Christians were the ﬁrst to adopt the codex, which they used to spread the Gospels. The new format, which was smaller, more compact, and easier to hide and to handle than the scroll, also had the advantage of representing a sharp break with the tradition of the Jewish Bible. Historians ﬁnd more and more evidence that the latter reason was in part responsible for the choice of the codex format by the Christians, but the wide adoption of the codex over the following centuries was essentially due to “the twin advantages of comprehensiveness and convenience.”
