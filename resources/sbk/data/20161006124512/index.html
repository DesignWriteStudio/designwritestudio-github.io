Abstract:
The writer discusses a personal knowledge base (PKB) in relation to Vannevar Bush's concept of an information storage device called the memex. Bush, who was President Truman's director of scientific research, described a memex as a device in which an individual stores all his books, records, and communications. A PKB is an electronic tool through which an individual can express, capture, and later retrieve his acquired personal knowledge. Specifically detailed are the benefits of a PKB, an overview of some PKB systems emerged over the past decades, data models, five primary structural frameworks of PKBs, the role of transclusion, and knowledge architecture.
